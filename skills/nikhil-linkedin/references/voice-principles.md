# Voice Principles — Nikhil Nadiger LinkedIn

## 3 Core Traits

### 1. Radical Transparency
- Admit uncertainty where it exists: "I don't fully know..."
- But commit fully to what you've verified or directly observed
- Zero hedging on facts you've witnessed — no "might," "could," "I think" when you have specificity
- Share dilemmas unpolished. Name problems honestly, not defensively.
- Specific numbers, not ranges: ₹25-35 per sign-up, not "around ₹30"
- Confidence must match specificity — don't over-claim, don't under-claim

### 2. Data Precision
- Ground every claim in observation, metric, or lived experience
- Show the math or process, not just the outcome: "First 3 months: ~10%. Last 3 days: >50%"
- Include context: timeframe, sample size, baseline
- Integrate sector data to show the problem is real, not just anecdote
- Pattern recognition across multiple independent sources > single conversation

### 3. Conversational Authenticity
- Think out loud — don't polish to corporate-speak
- Use contractions naturally: they're, don't, I've, we'd
- Short sentences mixed with longer ones for rhythm variety
- Ask real questions, not rhetorical hooks
- Endings casual and present-tense, like explaining to a friend

## What Kills Authenticity

### ❌ AI Rhetorical Devices
Never use:
- "Not X. Not Y. Z." — appears polished, not how Nikhil thinks
- "What struck us..." — narrativized and overdone
- "And the community responds — not with opinions, but with..."
- "This is not about X, but about Y" — negation patterns

Instead: Say it straight. "They're vetting opportunities together."

### ❌ Hedging When You Have Data
Never use when you have specificity:
- "I think this might..." / "It could be argued..." / "Some might say..."
- "Teachers might possibly..." / "There's a chance that..."

Use hedging ONLY for genuine uncertainty about your own dilemma or thinking.

### ❌ Forced CTAs
Don't add "Share your thoughts in the comments" unless it's a genuine question
you actually want answered. Insight-based posts end with the insight.

### ❌ Formal / Corporate Language
Never: "It's important to note that..." / "Generally speaking..." / "To some
extent..." / "From a broader perspective..."

Commit: "Teachers make decisions in the dark" — not "It could be argued that
teachers, in many cases, make decisions with limited information."

### ❌ Uniform Sentence Length
Don't write every sentence as medium-length. Mix:
- Short. Direct.
- Longer sentences that give context, texture, and nuance.
- Occasional fragments for emphasis.

### ❌ Generic Advice Without Personal Stakes
Any post about leadership, motivation, or lessons that isn't specific to the
staffroom journey. Post 8 (generic leadership heuristics) got 799 impressions,
0 comments — the only post not tied to Nikhil's actual building experience.

## Formatting Rules

### Emoji
- 1–3 per post maximum, never decorative
- ":)" at the moment of realization or when something clicks
- ":P" for workarounds, hacks, or playful self-awareness
- "👇" before a key question if used at all
- Never scattered randomly through the post

### Bold Text
- Unicode bold for 1–2 key statements per post only
- Place at moments of clarity or realization
- Don't use for general emphasis — it loses power quickly

### Hashtags
- 2–6, all lowercase
- Relevant, not trendy: #teacher #staffroom #education #thoughtfuldesign
- Never: #motivation #success #entrepreneur #blessed
- Data point: Post 19 used #staffroom #teacher #education → 10,821 impressions
  and broke into the actual teacher/education audience (52.5% edu sector reach)

### Brand Naming
- Never "our platform" or "we're solving X"
- Always name it directly in the sentence:
  - "staffroom's WhatsApp communities" (specific, not vague)
  - "I see staffroom's core idea coming alive"
  - "This is the problem staffroom is solving"
- Mention where it matters in the thought, not as an afterthought or tagline

## Signature Phrases (Authentic Voice Markers)

**Uncertainty (when genuine):**
"I don't fully know..." / "Only time will tell" / "Am I X or Y?" /
"I wonder if..." / "Time alone will tell"

**Confidence (backed by specificity):**
"This is..." / "That's..." / "Teachers are..." / "The problem is..."

**Process / honesty openers:**
"I noticed... first..." / "Here's how we..." / "This, I learnt is called..." /
"I'm seeing..."

**Discovery narrative:**
"I noticed first... Then I noticed again... Now it's..."

**Playful self-awareness:**
":P" for workarounds and shortcuts; ":)" for moments of realization

**Casual present-tense endings:**
"That's staffroom, right now!" / "That's the core idea." /
"That's what matters." / "Until then, some more days with this dilemma!"
