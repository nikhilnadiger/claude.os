# Post Patterns — Nikhil Nadiger LinkedIn

## Pattern 1: Direct Observation
**When:** You've noticed something real happening and want to name the pattern + why it matters.

Structure:
1. Open with direct observation: "I'm seeing X happening in [specific place]"
2. Ground it with specific details, examples, or verbatim quotes
3. Name the realization with emotional honesty (":)")
4. End with what it means: "This is the problem staffroom is solving"

Example (Post 19 — highest ever at 10,821 impressions):
> Teachers collaborate actively in staffroom WhatsApp communities. Teachers ask each other for
> school opportunities and they don't ask about salary first. They ask: Do they actually let
> you create lesson plans or do they give you a template?...
> I can see the core idea coming alive, in real-time :)

Key tells: Verbatim quotes from users, named emotion at realization moment, declarative ending

## Pattern 2: The Dilemma
**When:** You're genuinely stuck between two competing priorities and want to think out loud.

Structure:
1. Hook: Name the dilemma clearly with specific numbers/context
2. Middle: "On one side... On the other side..."
3. End: Admit uncertainty or ask a real question

Example (Post 10 — 4,737 impressions):
> I have ~8-9 months runway at current expenses. I don't fully know how economics of
> staffroom will work... On one side... On the other side...
> Am I a victim of the sunk cost fallacy, perseverant, lazy or something else?

Key tells: Specific timeframe, both sides named equally, self-questioning ending

## Pattern 3: Metrics + Process
**When:** You've achieved something measurable and want to show how you did it.

Structure:
1. Hook: "[Metric] in [timeframe]"
2. Middle: Numbered list of specific actions (not theory)
3. End: Ask for ideas or name the next question

Example (Post 11 — 2,117 impressions):
> We just 5x-ed our conversion rate in 72 hours. First 3 months: ~10%. Last 3 days: >50%.
> Here's how we broke the cold start cycle:
> 1. fetched reviews from Google, Naukri, Glassdoor
> 2. where unavailable, added AI-generated salaries and reviews
> 3. added UDISE data subset
> Any zero/low cost ideas for referral growth?

Key tells: Real before/after data, numbered steps, open-ended ask

## Pattern 4: Technical Transparency
**When:** Being honest about shortcuts, hacks, or the messy reality of building.

Structure:
1. Hook: "X is a mess" or "X worked, but..."
2. Middle: Evolution from simple to complex with specific examples
3. End: Name the unsolved question

Example (Post 12 — 1,540 impressions):
> staffroom's tech is a mess. It works, but is held together by manual checks, hacks and in
> places, just hope :P [evolution of the problem]
> I don't know where and when I should draw the line to separate the MVP from the actual product!

Key tells: Admit the problem, ":P" for playful acceptance, end with genuine uncertainty

## Pattern 5: Strategic Thinking
**When:** Exploring positioning, PMF, or long-term direction — showing the thinking, not conclusions.

Structure:
1. Hook: "What's [question] for [company]?"
2. Middle: Show your reasoning, not just the answer
3. End: Admit mixed opinions / honest uncertainty

Example (Post 17 — 78% comment:reaction ratio, highest deep engagement):
> What's PMF for staffroom?... I have now separated PMF and monetisation as two
> independent tracks. PMF I think will come city by city, maybe even pincode by pincode.
> Whether this is textbook PMF, I don't know. I read and hear mixed opinions.

Key tells: Invites collaboration, exposes reasoning, honest about ambiguity

## Pattern 6: Founder Anxiety (Emotional Honesty)
**When:** Working through a real feeling and wanting to normalize the uncertainty.

Structure:
1. Hook: Name the feeling directly
2. Middle: The trade-offs or dilemma it creates
3. End: Sit with uncertainty — no false resolution

Example (Post 13 — 2,202 impressions):
> Resisting the urge to work on staffroom is quite hard. I feel anxious, guilty and bored,
> all at once!... Discernment is a tough skill to hone. Only time will show the truth.

Key tells: Specific emotions named, no forced resolution, ends with acceptance

## Pattern 7: Sector Data / Partnership Angle
**When:** Sharing data or insights that matter for partners, investors, or sector observers.

Structure:
1. Hook: "[X%] of [sector] experience [Y]"
2. Middle: Context that makes it matter
3. End: Why staffroom exists for this

Example (Post 7 — 29.5% education sector reach, highest non-viral edu reach):
> staffroom is filling a gap in the schooling ecosystem... 50% pvt teachers are working without
> contracts. >85% pvt teachers get <Rs50k / month... Workplace environment → teacher well-being
> → student outcomes. This is known and yet, there is no mechanism or platform to know which
> schools are great workplaces.

Key tells: Specific statistics, problem named, solution named, chain logic visible

## Pattern 8: Multi-Source Validation
**When:** You've noticed a pattern across multiple independent sources and want to build credibility.

Structure:
1. State the observation
2. "I noticed first... [source A]"
3. "Then I noticed again... [source B]"
4. "Now it's happening... [source C — present]"
5. Name what this means

Key tells: Timeline progression (first → then → now), multiple independent validations,
pattern recognition not single anecdote

---

## Pattern 9: Data-Grounded Observation
**When:** Tying a personal observation to sector data to show it's a real market problem.

Structure:
1. State the observation
2. Ground it: "[X%] experience [Y]" or "[metric] confirms..."
3. Why this matters: "This is a critical issue because..."
4. How your solution addresses it

Key tells: Specific percentages (not "many" or "some"), direct connection between
observation and market scale, no fluff language

---

## Pattern 10: Market Inversion / Two-Sided Thinking
**When:** Taking an observation from one side of the market and asking "what does this mean
for the other side?"

Structure:
1. State observation about teachers/users
2. "And as an obvious extension..." — flip to schools/other side
3. Show how your solution serves both
4. Name what this unlocks

Key tells: "And as an obvious extension..." or "From the school perspective..."
Shows strategic two-sided thinking. Makes solution feel inevitable, not niche.

## Pattern 11: Personal Milestone
**When:** A transition, achievement, or chapter close with genuine stakes.

Structure:
1. Open with an emotional or narrative anchor (quote, question, observation)
2. Name the specific transition and what it took to get here
3. Acknowledge people genuinely — specific names, specific contributions
4. Name what you're carrying forward + where you're headed

Example (Post 1 — 5,647 impressions, 3.7% engagement rate, 114 profile viewers):
> lekin mujh ko hukm-e-safar hai, jaana hoga... A decade ago, I started out as a teacher
> with Teach For India... [names specific people with specific gratitude]
> I am setting out on my next decade equipped with this formula to build something in
> education with the potential to go global.

Key tells: Specific people named (drives network amplification), emotional anchor at start,
genuine gratitude without flattery, clear "what's next" — high profile viewer count signals
hire/investor intent from this pattern
