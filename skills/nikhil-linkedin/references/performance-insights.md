# Performance Principles — Nikhil LinkedIn

## How to Use the KPI Sheet

When deciding what to write or evaluating a draft, fetch the live KPI sheet
(in SKILL.md live_references). Look at:
- Impressions + Members Reached: raw size of audience reached
- Engagement Rate %: (reactions + comments + reposts) / impressions — quality signal
- Comment:Reaction Ratio %: comments / reactions — deep engagement signal
- Profile Viewers: hire / investor intent signal
- Edu Sector Reach %: whether you reached actual teachers or school-side audience
- Top Seniority / Top Location / Top Industry: who actually saw the post

---

## What Each Signal Means

**High impressions alone** = algorithm pushed it wide. Doesn't mean right audience.
Post 19 hit 10,821 impressions at 0.30% engagement — huge reach, shallow interaction.

**High engagement rate** = reached people who cared enough to respond.
Post 1 hit 5,647 impressions at 3.70% engagement — smaller reach, much deeper resonance.

**High comment:reaction ratio** = post sparked genuine thinking, not passive Like.
Anything above 30% is a strong signal investors and strategic thinkers are engaging.
Anything above 50% means the post was intellectually provocative or ambiguous enough
to require a response.

**High profile viewers** = people wanted to know who you are after reading.
This is the hire/investor intent signal. Peaks after personal, vulnerable, or milestone posts.

**High edu sector reach %** = post reached actual teachers or school administrators.
Default is 6–10%. Above 20% means the post broke out of the usual investor/founder bubble.

## Four Durable Patterns from the Data

**1. Hashtags are the lever for audience shift.**
Default audience is Senior, Delhi NCR, Business Consulting, Co-Founder. To reach actual
teachers or education sector, hashtags (#teacher #staffroom #education) are necessary.
Most high-impression posts used no hashtags. The post that broke to 10K+ used three.
Principle: use hashtags intentionally when the goal is partner/teacher reach, not by default.

**2. Personal stakes drive profile views; data drives comments.**
Vulnerability and personal transitions (milestone, anxiety, dilemma) make people want to
know who you are → profile clicks, hire intent.
Data-driven and strategic posts (metrics, PMF thinking) make people want to respond → comments,
investor signals. These are different goals. Don't conflate them.

**3. Generic advice without personal stakes has no audience.**
Every post that isn't tied to the actual staffroom-building journey underperforms.
The pattern is consistent across the full 19-post history. There's no exception to this.

**4. Deep engagement > total engagement for this account.**
A post with 7 comments and 9 reactions (78% ratio) from the right people is more valuable
than 100 reactions and 2 comments. The account's goal is to reach ~50 specific decision-makers,
not to go viral. Optimize for comment:reaction ratio and profile viewers, not raw impressions.
