# Channel Specs

Platform-by-platform rules for staffroom content. Each channel has
different format requirements, audience behaviour, and content fit.
Do not simply copy content across channels — adapt for each.

---

## Instagram Reels

**Status:** Paused as of Mar 2026. Existing posts/Reels remain live and
continue to accumulate views. Rules below apply when content resumes.

**Handle:** @thestaffroomdotin

**Primary use:** Awareness and reach. Validation hooks and Authority hooks
perform best here. Instagram is NOT a community platform for teachers
(Dovetail confirmed: teachers do not follow teaching creators on Instagram,
do not use it professionally). Treat it as a broadcast channel, not a
conversation channel.

**Format:**
- Duration: 30–75 seconds. 45–60 seconds is the proven sweet spot.
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 vertical, full screen
- Captions on screen: mandatory — most watched on mute
- Script format: use full IR table (Hook / Body / Closure)
- staffroom logo: bottom-right watermark throughout, or full-screen card at end

**Caption structure:**
```
[Opening line that mirrors the hook]

📌 [Key insight 1]
📌 [Key insight 2]
📌 [Key insight 3]

Based on [X] teacher experiences shared on staffroom.

Write your review → thestaffroom.in
Follow @thestaffroomdotin on Instagram

#teachers #indianteachers #teacherlife #[topic-specific hashtag]
```

**Hashtag guidance:** 3–5 hashtags maximum. Always include #teachers and
#indianteachers. Add 1–2 topic-specific tags. Do not stuff hashtags.

**Best-performing content types:** Validation hooks (Teachers Are TIRED:
1,217 shares), Authority hooks (NTA post: 261K views, 3,397 shares).
Data hooks also work for saves.

**What does not work:** Long captions without structure. Generic teaching
inspiration content. Content that requires sound to understand.

## YouTube Shorts

**Status:** Paused as of Mar 2026. Existing Shorts remain live and
continue to be discoverable. Rules below apply when content resumes.

**Handle:** @thestaffroom-in

**Primary use:** Discovery and subscriber acquisition. Data hooks perform
best — teachers search for specific information (salary, school type, tools).

**Format:**
- Duration: 45–60 seconds
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 vertical
- Script format: abbreviated IR table — Hook + 2–3 Body rows + Closure
- Captions: recommended for accessibility and muted viewing
- End screen: prompt to watch the long-form version if one exists

**Title requirements:**
- Must be searchable — write as a question the viewer is googling
- Include specific terms: school type (IB, IGCSE, CBSE), city (Bengaluru),
  subject (Math, English), or role (PGT, TGT, primary teacher)
- Good: "IB teacher salary India 2025 — the real numbers"
- Weak: "Teacher salary facts"

**Description structure:**
```
[1–2 sentence summary of what the video covers]

Key points:
• [Point 1 with specific number or detail]
• [Point 2]
• [Point 3]

Share your school experience: thestaffroom.in
Subscribe to @thestaffroom-in on YouTube.
```

**Shorts → Long-form funnel:** Shorts should serve as teasers for long-form
content where it exists. End the Short with: "Full breakdown on the channel."

## YouTube Long-Form

**Status:** Paused as of Mar 2026. Existing videos remain live and
continue to accumulate views and subscribers. Rules below apply when
content resumes.

**Handle:** @thestaffroom-in

**Primary use:** Deep engagement, trust-building, subscriber retention. Career
& Salary content drives the highest completion rates (IB: 74.49%, IGCSE:
73.54%). Long-form is where staffroom builds credibility as the authoritative
source on Indian teacher career data.

**Format:**
- Duration: 8–14 minutes for Career & Salary. 5–10 minutes for Teaching
  Practice and Wellbeing. No artificial padding to hit a duration — end
  when the content ends.
- Aspect ratio: 16:9 landscape
- Structure: Hook (0–30s) → Context/Setup (30s–2min) → Core Content
  (2min–end) → CTA + staffroom card (last 30s)
- Chapters: add timestamp chapters in description for videos over 8 minutes

**Script format:** Use the long-form outline template in
`references/script-style-guide.md` (Long-Form Script Format section). It
includes the full Outline table, Description template, title rules, and
length targets.

**Thumbnail:** Every long-form video needs a thumbnail — brief it using
visual-asset-creation. The thumbnail formula and prompt template are in
`visual-asset-creation/references/gemini-assets.md` (Asset Type 1).

## LinkedIn (staffroom Company Page)

**Status:** Paused as of Mar 2026. Company page exists. Rules below apply
when activated.

**Primary use:** B2B credibility — school principals, HR leads, education
sector partners, investors. Different audience from Instagram/YouTube. This
is NOT teacher-facing content; it is school/partner-facing.

**Tone shift from other channels:** More formal than Instagram/YouTube but
still warm. Remove Hinglish. Use data-led, professional register. staffroom
as a platform, not a teacher advocacy voice.

**Content types for LinkedIn:**
- Platform milestones: "635 teachers have shared their experiences on
  staffroom across 555 schools in India."
- Data insights framed for school leaders: "Schools that respond to teacher
  feedback are rated 40% higher on staffroom. Here's what that looks like."
  [hypothetical — use real data when available]
- Partnership and recognition announcements
- Thought leadership from Nikhil on the Indian private school teacher market
  (use nikhil-linkedin skill for Nikhil's personal posts — this is for the
  company page)

**Format:**
- Post length: 150–300 words. LinkedIn rewards native long-form but
  staffroom's company page should keep posts tight until it builds a following.
- No script table needed — write as a LinkedIn post with line breaks for
  readability
- No hashtag stuffing: 3–5 hashtags maximum (#EducationIndia #Teachers
  #SchoolLeadership)
- Images: include a data visual or platform screenshot where relevant.
  Use visual-asset-creation for LinkedIn data cards.

**What not to post on company LinkedIn:**
- Teacher grievance content — wrong audience, wrong tone
- Generic inspiration quotes
- Reposts of Instagram Reels without adaptation

## WhatsApp Broadcast

**Status:** Paused as of Mar 2026. Groups remain active with existing
members. Rules below apply when broadcast content resumes.
Three groups: Announcements (broadcast), Open Group (two-way), Teachers Hub
(job board dynamics).

**Primary use:** Direct teacher communication — platform updates, nudges to
share experiences, useful content drops. Not for long-form content.

**Format:**
- Length: 3–5 sentences maximum. Teachers are on mobile, in between classes.
- No script table — write as a message
- Tone: personal and direct. "Hi, one quick thing for you today." not
  "Dear Community Members,"
- Hinglish welcome here — WhatsApp is the most informal channel
- No hashtags
- CTA: one per message, clear and specific: "Tap here to share your
  experience: [link]"

**Content fit for WhatsApp:**
- Short, useful information (salary fact, teaching tip, school insight)
- Platform nudges ("We have [X] experiences. Yours will help 10 more teachers.")
- Event or milestone announcements
- Poll questions to the Open Group

**Content that does not fit WhatsApp:**
- Long-form scripts or articles
- Complex data breakdowns
- Marketing messages that feel broadcast-y — WhatsApp intimacy is its value;
  exploit it carefully

**Group-specific notes:**
- Announcements group: one-way, broadcast. Use for platform updates and
  content drops. Keep to 1–2 messages per week maximum.
- Open Group: two-way. Teachers respond here. Polls and questions work well.
  Community manager response needed after any engagement prompt.
- Teachers Hub: job-board behaviour. Salary data and job tips land well here.

## Meta Ads (Paid)

**Status:** Active (running as of Mar 2026 on Home_Conversion and BLR campaigns).

**Primary use:** Paid acquisition — driving teachers to the staffroom homepage
to sign up or write a review.

**What the data shows (MetaAds-by-content-metrics.csv, Mar 2026):**

- **Salary intent converts 40% cheaper than reviews intent:** CPR ₹98
  (salary) vs ₹162 (reviews). When running paid acquisition, salary-led
  creative is the more efficient entry point.
- **Kannada-English creatives outperform English-only by ~2x** in the
  Bengaluru-focused campaign: CPR ₹144 (KaEn) vs ₹279 (English-only).
  Bilingual creative is not optional for Bengaluru targeting — it is a
  performance requirement.
- **AI Avatar format works:** CPR ₹101 — better than English-only video.
  Worth scaling and testing against other formats.
- **Video play time (VPT) correlates with performance:** The highest-
  performing ad (Ad7) had 7.1s average VPT and the most total conversions.
  The hook must retain the viewer past 5 seconds. Ads with near-zero VPT
  (non-video or very short) underperformed.
- **Carousel format:** Insufficient data (₹39 spend). Do not conclude
  anything from this yet.

**Creative format guidance:**
- Short video (≤30s) with a strong hook in the first 3s
- Bilingual (Kannada/Hinglish + English) for Bengaluru targeting
- Salary-led or school-reality-led content for conversion campaigns
- Reviews-led content for awareness/reach campaigns (higher CPR but
  broader engagement: shares, saves)
- AI Avatar format is viable — test at higher budgets

## Cross-Channel Adaptation Rules

When adapting a piece of content across channels, the following always changes:

| Element | Instagram Reel | YouTube Short | YouTube Long | LinkedIn | WhatsApp |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Duration | 45–75s | 45–60s | 8–14min | N/A | 3–5 sentences |
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 | 9:16 | 16:9 | Square/landscape | N/A |
| Hinglish | Yes | Yes | Yes | No | Yes |
| Hashtags | 3–5 | 3–5 | 3–5 | 3–5 | None |
| Script table | Yes (IR) | Yes (abbreviated) | Yes (full outline) | No | No |
| CTA | Share experience | Subscribe | Subscribe | Partnership/follow | Direct link |
| Thumbnail | Cover frame | Auto-generated | Custom thumbnail | Post image | N/A |
