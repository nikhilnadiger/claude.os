## Piyush Pandey (Indian) — Ogilvy India

**Key principle:** Dream like a child, strategize like an adult.
**When to invoke:** Brand voice, Indian cultural positioning, campaigns, emotional truth, conviction-led decisions.

- Childlike instinct first, strategy second — not the reverse
- Humor is a permission structure, not a dilution of authority
- Rural India contains universal human truths; mine them with urban craft
- Fight for one conviction; committees produce mediocrity
- Emotions are a spectrum — match the right one to the truth, not just sentiment
- Respect the audience's intelligence in every decision
- The brief is where 80% of the real work happens — challenge it
- Craft is how you show respect; poor execution is a broken promise
- Culture is a bigger arena than category — plug into what India is actually thinking
- Discomfort from stakeholders often signals you've found something true

## Prasoon Joshi (Indian) — McCann India

**Key principle:** Poetry is not decoration — it is business logic.
**When to invoke:** Language, authenticity, cultural sentiment, Hinglish, emotional resonance, simplicity vs. complexity debates.

- Language carries worldview; word choice is strategic, not cosmetic
- Sentiment mapping: track the emotional undercurrent of a culture, not just its surface mood
- Simple ideas aren't shallow — complexity of thought expressed simply is the hardest skill
- Poetry and commerce are not opposites; a line that lasts is an asset
- Authenticity beats aspiration when the audience can smell the difference
- Indian storytelling has its own grammar — don't borrow Western narrative arcs wholesale
- The brand's relationship with silence and restraint is as important as what it says
- Contradictions in culture are not problems to resolve — they are truths to honour
- Music, rhythm, and meter in copy are not stylistic choices; they affect retention
- A brand that reflects the culture back to itself creates belonging, not just preference

## Santosh Desai (Indian) — Futurebrands / Cultural Commentator

**Key principle:** Brands that understand India's internal contradictions outlast those that flatten them.
**When to invoke:** India market dynamics, cultural complexity, consumer insight, brand evolution over time, urban-rural tensions.

- Indian consumers hold multiple, contradictory identities simultaneously — honour all of them
- Aspiration in India is not about leaving one's roots; it is about elevating them
- The gap between what people say and what they do is the most valuable research gap
- Culture changes in layers — surface trends move fast, deep values move slowly; know which you're touching
- Brands built on functional superiority alone are fragile in India; emotional belonging is the moat
- Internal brand culture and external brand promise must be congruent — gaps show
- Brand evolution requires knowing what is non-negotiable in the original identity
- The real competition is not other brands; it is people's existing habits and mental models
- Marketing that tries to resolve cultural tension dishonestly loses trust permanently
- Localism is not a constraint; it is a source of competitive distinctiveness

## Arindam Paul (Indian) — Atomberg / Zero to Scale

**Key principle:** PPCMF, not PMF — in India, brand building is re-earned at every new price point, channel, and geography.
**When to invoke:** Consumer brand scaling, distribution strategy, performance marketing, product-market fit, India GTM, pricing, omnichannel org design, innovation filters, brand measurement.

- Indian consumers are value-conscious, not price-conscious — make the math of value obvious, don't cut the price
- Design is now a default expectation in every category, not a premium signal — aesthetics drive decisions even in functional goods
- New India buys on excitement first; early adopters will try unknown brands if the social proof threshold is met
- Win the evaluation phase (reviews, explainers, search) — awareness without evaluation presence doesn't convert
- Good supply unlocks TAM; don't cap your market estimate on current category size
- Service is your cheapest acquisition channel in early-stage — fix one problem well, the customer tells five people
- PPCMF must be re-achieved for every new city, channel, and price tier — PMF is never a one-time milestone in India
- Find your wedge first — smallest viable market where you can win deeply, then expand
- Separate brand campaigns from performance campaigns — mixing objectives dilutes both
- Know which of the three growth levers (product / distribution / brand) is actually driving your growth before doubling down

## Rory Sutherland (Global) — Ogilvy UK / Behavioural Economics

**Key principle:** The most valuable real estate is in the human mind, not the market.
**When to invoke:** Pricing psychology, perceived value vs. actual value, counterintuitive positioning, behaviour change, reframing problems.
*Note: Extrapolates from Western/UK behavioral economics. Flag when applying to Indian private school teacher context.*

- Logic solves the wrong problem; reframe before optimising
- Perceived value and actual value are different levers — both are real
- The opposite of a good idea can also be a good idea; explore both poles
- Psycho-logic: human choices follow emotional coherence, not rational maximisation
- Friction is sometimes the product — removing it destroys the signal of value
- Context changes meaning more than content does
- Small interventions in the choice environment outperform large campaigns
- Brands with genuine idiosyncrasy are more trusted than those optimised for universal appeal
- The reason something works is rarely the reason people think it works
- Inconvenience, scarcity, and effort create perceived worth — use deliberately
