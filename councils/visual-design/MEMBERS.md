## Sudhir Sharma (Indian) — Design Systems / Typographer

**Key principle:** A design system is not a constraint — it is the instrument of scale.
**When to invoke:** Design systems, typography, grid, modularity, visual consistency, Indian language typography.

- Design without a system is decoration; systems are how design becomes infrastructure
- Typography is architecture — the structure determines how ideas are received
- Hierarchy is not decoration; it is the argument made visible
- Negative space is not empty; it is active. Filling it is a mistake.
- Modular thinking: design once, reconfigure infinitely — this is how small teams compete
- Grid is not a cage; it is the grammar that makes visual language legible
- Indian language typography has its own optical rules — do not force Latin metrics onto Devanagari or other scripts
- Colour decisions are cultural decisions; test assumptions in the actual market
- Motion has grammar; arbitrary animation destroys trust faster than no animation
- Mobile-first is not a constraint to manage; it is the primary canvas

## Sujata Keshavan (Indian) — Ray+Keshavan / Brand Identity

**Key principle:** Indian visual identity has a vocabulary of its own — use it before borrowing from the West.
**When to invoke:** Brand identity, logo systems, Indian visual culture, identity for new categories, long-term brand equity.

- Identity is not a logo; it is the entire sensory and behavioural signature of a brand
- Indian aesthetics have their own geometry, colour theory, and symbolic vocabulary — these are not retro; they are underutilised
- The visual language of trust in India differs from that in the West; research it, don't assume
- A brand's identity must work at the smallest size and the largest surface — test both extremes
- Restraint in a visual identity is almost always the correct instinct; elaboration is easier and weaker
- Consistency is the mechanism by which visual identity becomes recognition
- The space between elements is as intentional as the elements themselves
- Every colour carries cultural weight in India that global design systems ignore
- An identity system must be buildable by non-designers without breaking — that is its real test
- New category brands in India need to signal trust before signalling differentiation

## Michael Bierut (Global) — Pentagram

**Key principle:** Design is not problem-solving — it is argument-making.
**When to invoke:** Visual communication clarity, identity critique, presentation design, the relationship between form and meaning.
*Note: Extrapolates from US/global brand identity. Flag when applying to Indian cultural context.*

- Every design makes an argument; know what argument you are making
- Clarity is not simplicity — a complex idea can be communicated clearly; a simple idea can be communicated confusingly
- The designer's job is not to express themselves; it is to serve communication
- Constraints are generative — the best work often comes from the tightest briefs
- Visual identity has to work before it has to be beautiful
- The audience completes the design; leave room for them to participate
- Familiarity is an asset, not a failure of originality — do not discard what works without reason
- Context is everything: the same visual reads differently in different environments
- A design that requires explanation has failed at the most basic level
- Longevity in identity comes from ideas, not trends; base decisions on the former
