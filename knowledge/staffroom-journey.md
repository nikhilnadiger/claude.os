# staffroom — Company Journey

## Diagnosis and Discovery

The journey of the staffroom began with an ethnographic study across India. We interacted with 30-35 schools and 100+ teachers across urban, rural, government, private, regions and income levels. The goal was to understand the human beings operating within the education structure.

The primary discovery was that teachers are optimizing for their professional lives and careers. They make decisions based on work culture, pay scales, opportunities, management support etc. A median school teacher is 34yrs old, married, and has kids and/or aging parents. This stands in contrast to existing solutions, which often view teachers as "last mile delivery agents of impact."

An observation we made was that three apps were on every teacher's phone: YouTube, Instagram, and WhatsApp. Hence, we started out as a social media community by creating content focused on teachers as professionals. We featured teachers sharing their journeys, jobs, mentorship etc.

This phase was also when experimented rapidly. One of the early experiments was where we linked ChatGPT to WhatsApp to help teachers create lesson plans, classroom activities and draft leave letters. After  testing ideas coming from the now 12k+ community, we landed at the current product.

#### Design and MVP

#### The crux of what we have learnt is that schools are poor workplaces. This is also the reason why most solutions and ideas have not diffused into every classroom. Consider 4L+ private schools which account for half the kids (12cr) and half the teachers (50L).

On one side are teachers:   
\- 50% of teachers in private schools work without written contracts  
\- 54% of private schools don’t have functional toilets for staff and 47% don’t have a staffroom  
\- 57% of private school teachers are ineligible for job benefits like maternity leave, PF etc.  
\- Delayed salary is the norm; some teachers return part of their bank-transferred salary in cash-form

On the other hand are schools: 90%+ private schools charge \<₹2,000/month  
\- 20-30% attrition every year is the norm in private schools  
\- 32% of D.Ed/D.El.Ed and 38% of B.Ed graduates pass TET; only 1.5% secure \>70% marks  
\- \>35% of RTE reimbursements are not approved and settlements delayed by months or years  
\- \~60% of private schools report that fee collection from parents is a major challenge

Global research and lived experience is clear. Great workplaces improve teacher well-being which directly impacts student outcomes. Yet, there is no platform that credibly spotlights schools which are great workplaces. The best and probably the only way to discover such schools is via teachers.

We built an MVP and found the response to be encouraging. \~20% of teachers who sign up share their experience including salary, voluntarily, and anonymously. Sign-ups came from 12k+ community, MetaAds at ₹30/sign-up and referrals. 2500+ sign ups so far.

Inbound requests and emerging business model

Teachers reached out via Instagram, WhatsApp, Email and YouTube to get career guidance.

One of the private school teachers quit last year to prepare for a government job. The government hiring cycle is unclear now. She feels underconfident in applying to private schools based on chats with friends. She reached out for help with a demo class but she needed executive coaching.

Similarly, school owners/principals reached out to seek support to improve / grow the school.

One of the private school owners started a new school in 2023 after selling her previous one. She now feels like shutting down this one as it no longer is in line with the values she started out with. Further, she faces cash-flow issues which lead her to expensive personal loans. She lacks mentors, a strong team and a structured workplan. She reached out for help with improving admissions but needed executive coaching and links to existing solutions including working capital financing.

We are curating existing solutions, businesses, organisations and financial institutions to connect them to teachers and/or schools. The businesses value us because we give them a hot lead (not warm) and schools/teachers value us because they now gain a sense of choice and agency.

For one of the schools, we built an AI tool to automate hiring decisions. Instead of in-person demo classes, schools request videos that are uploaded to the tool. They get a 4 page report with an executive summary based on their metrics like vocabulary, presence, scaffolding of content etc. When we demo-ed it, we learnt that the same tool can be used for coaching and training.

Revenue channels and path ahead

Product \- \>70% of the searches by teachers in a city/pincode must take them to a school with at least 3 experiences already present. Then, checking staffroom before joining/applying or even negotiating becomes the default behaviour for every teacher in that city/pincode. We can then get schools to pay for adding an “Apply to us” button on their page, integrating our AI tool to automate hiring assessment, running ads for jobs, and access to working capital loans. We grow city by city.

Partnerships \- we are working with 10 organisations / businesses as a pilot to help them partner with a school. Based on the learning from the pilot, we plan to create a revenue channel where businesses get access to a list of schools that are curated to fit their needs.

Coaching and shared services \- most schools do not need and cannot afford full-time experts like coaches, extra-curricular teachers etc. Based on the need for executive coaching, we plan to pilot a shared services model where 5-10 schools get 1 coach or experts like PT teachers.

GTM and Brand \- an annual, credible and aspirational “great staffroom to work” award for schools that are rated highly by at least 5 teachers on staffroom and finalised by a power-packed jury. In the first year, we plan to get 1000+ schools to participate and award Top-25 across income levels.
