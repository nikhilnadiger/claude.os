### What the engagement signals mean

**Saves** = "I want to come back to this" = deep utility or emotional resonance. "Why Don't Students Follow Rules?" (2,208 saves) is a practical teaching problem post — saves signal teachers found it actionable.

**Shares** = "I want someone else to see this" = social identity signal or strong resonance. "Teachers Are TIRED" (1,217 shares on only 35K views = 3.5% share rate) is exceptional. Teachers shared it because it named their experience. The NTA post (3,397 shares on 261K views = 1.3%) was amplified by the national award hook.

**Views alone** are a weak signal — the NTA post likely had high organic reach due to topicality. The "Teachers Are TIRED" post is a stronger signal because it generated high shares despite lower reach.

### Hook type performance

| Hook type | Example | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Authority Hook | "National Teacher Award Winner's Take" | 261K views, 3,397 shares — reach driven by authority credibility |
| Validation Hook | "Teachers Are TIRED" | 35K views but 1,217 shares — highest share rate; naming the experience drives sharing |
| Data Hook | School-switching frequency data | Saves + shares; teachers recognise the data as real |

**Pattern:** Validation hooks generate the highest share-per-view rate. Authority hooks generate raw reach. Data hooks generate saves (utility) and shares (recognition of truth).

### Mission-aligned content note

"Why Do Teachers Change Schools Frequently" is the most directly aligned to staffroom's product thesis — school workplace quality drives teacher mobility. Despite lower absolute numbers (26K views), it generated meaningful shares (380) and maps directly to what staffroom exists to answer. This category of content should be expanded.

### Meta Ads — Strategic Conclusions (Apr 2026)

1. **Salary content drives cheapest conversions (CPR ₹26–28)** — consistent across ad creative (AI Avatar), campaign type (View Salary), and prospecting (Pr_View Salaries CPR ₹44). YouTube data agrees (74% completion on salary videos). Scale salary-first creative.

2. **Reviews content drives the most sharing (494 shares from See Reviews_Campaign Ad6)** — different mechanism than salary. Salary = personal utility (teachers save it for themselves); reviews = social signal (teachers share it to help peers). Both matter but serve different roles.

3. **AI Avatar format outperforms all others on efficiency + VPT.** CPR ₹26 + 7.2s VPT with ₹11,607 spend = proven at meaningful scale. Not a fluke.

4. **Phone-Verification goal (CPR ₹28–32) is dramatically more efficient than Home page goal (CPR ₹65–159).** When structuring future campaigns, use Phone-Verification as the conversion event where possible.

5. **BLR campaign is expensive and should be reassessed.** ₹30,773 spent for 194 results at CPR ₹159 — more than 5× worse than View Salary. Bengaluru localisation adds cost without proportional efficiency gains at current scale.

## Cross-Platform Content Pillar Performance

> Canonical pillar definitions, format principles, and topic ideas live in
> `skills/content-strategy/references/content-pillars.md`. This section
> shows the performance evidence that validates each pillar.

These 5 pillars are derived from the cross-reference of YouTube completion rates, Instagram saves/shares, and Dovetail research on what teachers want to learn and share.

### Pillar 1: Career & Salary
**Evidence:** Highest YouTube views and completion rates (IB salary 74.49%, IGCSE 73.54%). NTA post (261K views) driven by career achievement hook. Teachers actively search for salary data — it doesn't exist credibly elsewhere.
**Format that works:** Specific numbers, named school types, named roles. Not "teachers earn X on average" — "an IB Math teacher at a Bengaluru international school earns ₹X–Y."

### Pillar 2: Teaching Practice
**Evidence:** AI tools video (3,417 views, 69.73% completion). Saves on student behaviour posts. Dovetail: all 3 segments want practical classroom techniques.
**Format that works:** "Here's what worked and why." Specific examples grounded in Indian classroom context, not generic pedagogy. Segment 1 wants low-resource solutions; Segments 2–3 want innovative pedagogy.

### Pillar 3: School Workplace Reality
**Evidence:** "Why Do Teachers Change Schools Frequently" (mission-aligned, meaningful shares). "Teachers Are TIRED" (highest share rate). Directly maps to staffroom's product thesis.
**Format that works:** Naming the reality without exploiting it. Data-backed ("X% of teachers in staffroom's research say..."). Validation hook is the strongest format for this pillar.
**Risk to manage:** Avoid accusatory tone toward schools. staffroom does not take sides — it surfaces reality.

### Pillar 4: Teacher Wellbeing
**Evidence:** "Teachers Are TIRED" (1,217 shares — teachers shared because it named their experience). Dovetail: emotional spillover, recognition hunger, idealism-reality gap are live pain points.
**Format that works:** Validation of specific, named experiences — not generic "teaching is hard." Hinglish authenticity helps. Sugandhi's quote about being seen by her HM is a content template in itself.

### Pillar 5: Platform Data Reveals
**Evidence:** No direct performance data yet — this is an untapped format. staffroom now has 521+ live reviews, salary medians, rating distributions, and school-specific patterns that no one else has access to.
**Format that works:** "staffroom data shows..." hooks. Specific, surprising, India-specific. This pillar is a direct brand differentiator and should be built out as review volume grows.
